What is IVUS (Intravascular Ultrasound) and why is it better?

Intravascular ultrasound is a medical imaging methodology using a specially designed catheter with a miniaturized ultrasound probe attached to the distal end of the catheter. The proximal end of the catheter is attached to computerized ultrasound equipment.

Benefits

  • Increased accuracy of stent placement
  • Real-time evaluation
  • Reduced complications
  • faster recovery
  • Better quality of life

Department of Cardiology at Sakra World Hospital

All cardiologists study the disorders of the heart, but the study of adult and child heart disorders are through different training pathways. Therefore, an adult cardiologist (often simply called "cardiologist") is inadequately trained to take care of children.

The surgical aspects are not included in cardiology and are in the domain of cardiothoracic surgery. For example, coronary artery bypass surgery (CABG), cardiopulmonary bypass and valve replacement are surgical procedures performed by surgeons not cardiologists. However the insertion of stents and pacemakers is performed by cardiologists
